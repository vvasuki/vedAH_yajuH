Eggeling
  1. With (Vāj. S. XIX, 7), ‘Separately, indeed, a seat, acceptable to the gods, hath been prepared for you two,’ he fills the (three) cups

of Surā-liquor; for separate, indeed, are the Soma-juice and the Surā-liquor; and ‘acceptable to the gods’ he says, because these two are indeed acceptable to the gods; and ‘separately a seat hath been prepared’ he says, because there are two altar-grounds;–‘do not ye mingle in the highest heaven!’ he thereby keeps him (the Sacrificer) from evil;–’the potent Surā-liquor thou art,’ he thereby makes Surā to be Surā;–‘and this is Soma,’ he thereby makes Soma to be Soma;–’entering thine own seat, injure me not!’ he thereby turns it (the Surā-liquor) away to its own seat for his own safety. With a single (verse) he fills them: singly and solely on the Sacrificer he thus bestows fame, for the Surā-liquor is fame.
