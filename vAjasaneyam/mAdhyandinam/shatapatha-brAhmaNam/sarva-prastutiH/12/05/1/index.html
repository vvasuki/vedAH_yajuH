  16. 201:1 The burning of the dead body seems to be compared here with the cutting up of the victim which is done outside the sacrificial ground. Harisvāmin, indeed, takes ‘grāma’ here, not in the sense of ‘village,’ but in that of ‘agnisamūha’–in the midst of the (set of) sacrificial fires–which, if it were possible, would certainly make the comparison even more striking. ↩︎
