Eggeling
  1. He then takes (ghee) in sixteen ladlings: eight vital airs, and eight limbs 3,–this (the symbolical) amount. He takes it in the same spoon, for, indeed, the vital airs and the limbs are in the same body. Separately 4 he offers: he thereby makes a distinction between the vital airs and the limbs. With two (verses) addressed to Vi≈õvakarman he offers: Vi≈õvakarman is this Agni, it is him he thus puts together. Three oblations he offers,–threefold is Agni: as great as Agni is, as great as is his measure, with so much food he thus restores him. With seventeen verses (he offers) 5,–PrajƒÅpati is seventeenfold, and PrajƒÅpati is Agni: as great as Agni is, as great as is his measure, with so much he thus restores him.

With (ghee) taken in twenty-one ladlings (he offers the two oblations),–there are twelve months, five seasons, these three worlds, and yonder sun as the twenty-first: this is the (symbolical) amount (or, correspondence).
